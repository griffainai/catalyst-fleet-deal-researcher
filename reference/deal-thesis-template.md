# reference/deal-thesis-template.md — The Output Shape

Every full piece of research collapses to this. It is a *thesis*, not a profile. The defining feature is section 5 — kill-conditions — without which it's a summary wearing a costume.

---

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════
DEAL THESIS — [Fleet Name]
Angle: [open / qualify / call-prep / walk decision]
Date: [today — because the facts decay]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════

1. THE CLAIM (one sentence)
   "[Fleet] is a [buy-now / qualify-up / deprioritize] target
    because [catalyst], and the signer is [role/name + confidence]."

2. THE CATALYST  (why now ≠ six months ago)
   - Family: [post-incident / labor / ownership / regulatory-insurance]
   - Event: [specific, dated]
   - Source tier: [T1–T4]
   - Window status: [open / closing / spent]
   - If none: "No live catalyst. Recommend deprioritize."

3. THE SIGNER
   - Role: [VP Safety / CFO / CEO / ...]
   - Name: [confirmed name | "Unknown — close via X"]
   - Confidence: [confirmed / likely / inferred]

4. THE LEDGER
   KNOWN     (T1–2, sourced + dated): ...
   INFERRED  (T3–4, labeled):         ...
   UNKNOWN   (the gaps + how to close each): ...

5. THREE FINDINGS THAT WOULD KILL THIS THESIS   ← mandatory
   1. [disconfirming finding] → [what we'd do]
   2. ...
   3. ...

6. THE NEXT QUESTION (single highest-leverage unknown)
   - Question: ...
   - Why it decides the deal: ...
   - Fastest way to answer it: ...
═══════════════════════════════════════════════
```

---

## Why each section exists

- **Claim** forces a position. A researcher takes a stance; a summarizer reports.
- **Catalyst** is the urgency test. No catalyst, no priority.
- **Signer** prevents the wrong-first-touch mistake.
- **Ledger** keeps knowledge, inference, and ignorance from blurring together.
- **Kill-conditions** are what make the thesis *falsifiable* — the heart of the whole tool. If you can't write three, you don't understand the account yet.
- **Next question** is the deliverable's payload: in a world of cheap answers, the right *question* is the scarce thing.

---

## The call-prep variant (compressed)

When the operator is <24h from a call, drop the full template and give four lines only:
1. **The catalyst** (your "why now") — dated, tiered.
2. **The signer in the seat** — confidence-labeled.
3. **The opening question** (not a pitch) — one that makes them name the catalyst themselves.
4. **The objection to expect** + the one-line counter.

Keep the source discipline even when compressing: if the load-bearing fact is volatile and unre-checked, say so.
